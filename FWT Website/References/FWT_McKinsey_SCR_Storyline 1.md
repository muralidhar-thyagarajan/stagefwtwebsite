— [T2] Server-grade DRAM prices spiked 50% in 2025 and may double again in 2026 — direct cost pressure on hosted-AI consumption. (TrueFoundry)
— [T3] The "1,000 ChatGPT instances" anti-pattern — 50 associates running 50 separate ChatGPT instances to do the same job — illustrates how AI adoption without a business case quietly burns money. (Internal source: Flywheel Tech Park.md)

## Logical-Validity Check (the Governing Thought test)

The Governing Thought — _"Mid-size US BFSI firms should partner with FWT to stand up a Pune Micro-GCC on a BOT basis"_ — is the conjoint conclusion of the four supporting arguments below. Each argument is necessary; none alone is sufficient.

1. **AI is now competitive necessity** (Argument 1) → you must act.
2. **Default approaches fail** (Argument 2) → you cannot act through APIs + consultancies alone.
3. **A Pune Micro-GCC is the right vehicle** (Argument 3) → here is the operating model.
4. **FWT is the right partner** (Argument 4) → here is who executes it.

Remove any one argument and the recommendation collapses (e.g., without Argument 2 the reader could rationally choose an OpenAI-plus-Big-5 path; without Argument 3 the reader could rationally pick a 30-person managed-services contract). The four arguments together are MECE on the decision the customer must make.
