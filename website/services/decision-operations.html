SERVICES / DECISION OPERATIONS

DECISION OPERATIONS

AI-Enabled. Human-Powered. Audit-Ready.

The operational layer where AI decisions meet human judgment — and where regulated financial institutions protect themselves.

In regulated financial services, AI cannot make consequential decisions autonomously. Adverse action requirements, fair lending obligations, model risk management guidelines, and the operational reality of exception handling all mandate a structured Human-in-the-Loop governance layer — not as a temporary concession to regulators, but as a permanent operational architecture.

Decision Operations is the name for this layer. It is where AI output meets human review — where uncertain decisions are escalated, where confidence thresholds are managed, where the audit trail that regulators require is maintained, and where the feedback loop that improves your AI models over time is closed.

THE REGULATORY MANDATE

CFPB. OCC. EU AI Act. Fannie Mae. The Mandate for HITL Is Already Enforced.

The institutions operating AI in regulated lending without a documented HITL governance layer are accumulating a compliance liability that is already being measured.

The regulatory landscape for AI in financial services has crystallised rapidly. CFPB adverse action requirements mandate that every automated lending decision be explainable to the applicant in plain language. OCC Bulletin 2026-13 updates model risk management guidance to include generative AI and agentic systems. EU AI Act Article 6 classifies credit scoring and underwriting AI as high-risk — with mandatory human oversight requirements. Fannie Mae LL-2026-04 requires sellers and servicers to apply AI governance standards "no less protective" than existing model risk frameworks.

These are current obligations. They apply specifically to the AI systems that are most valuable in financial services — the ones making credit, underwriting, and compliance decisions. Decision Operations is the operational architecture that satisfies these requirements — not as a compliance checkbox, but as a functioning capability.

THE CAPABILITY

Oversight. Escalation. Provenance. Feedback. The Four Functions That Make AI Trustworthy.

Decision Operations is not one thing. It is four coordinated functions that together make AI trustworthy in regulated environments.

Oversight

Trained human reviewers assess escalated AI decisions — those below confidence thresholds, outside policy guardrails, or flagged by the AI system itself. Reviewers are trained on your specific decision domains, regulatory requirements, and escalation criteria.

Escalation management

The escalation architecture defines which decisions require human review, at what confidence thresholds, and with what response time requirements. This is designed in the Advisory phase and refined continuously as the AI system matures.

Decision provenance

Every AI-assisted decision — the model, the inputs, the confidence score, the escalation outcome, the reviewer identity and rationale — is documented in an auditable record. This is the audit trail that satisfies regulatory examination.

Feedback loop

Reviewer decisions and rationale feed back into model training and refinement. Over time, the AI system becomes more accurate — and the escalation volume decreases — because the human review layer is continuously improving the models it oversees.

BROADER APPLICABILITY

Document-Driven, Decision-Intensive Environments Across Industries.

The same operating architecture applies wherever regulated decisions depend on documents, rules, and human judgment.

The Decision Operations capability was built for BFSI — but the operating architecture applies wherever regulated transactions progress through a defined lifecycle, relying on documents, rules, human judgment, and auditable decision-making to reach an outcome.

Legal services. Healthcare record adjudication. Insurance claims. Title and escrow. Government benefit determinations. The specific regulatory requirements differ. The core operating architecture — oversight, escalation, provenance, feedback — is the same.

For organisations outside BFSI exploring this capability, the starting point is identical: an Advisory engagement to map the decision domains, assess the regulatory requirements, and design the right HITL architecture for your specific environment.
